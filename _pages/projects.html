Spinal Cord Injury in Bangladesh (2011–2021): An 11-Year Single-Centre Epidemiological Study

Summary structured using the STAR Method

  1. SITUATION (The Condition):

    The project addressed the significant burden and lack of current epidemiological data for **Spinal Cord Injury (SCI)** in developing nations. The specific condition was analyzing the retrospective data of **3,575 individuals with SCI** admitted to a major rehabilitation center in Bangladesh over an 11-year period (2011–2021).

  2. TASK (Data Processing):

    I was tasked with performing the **data cleaning, manipulation, and cleaning** necessary to transform raw patient records into a structured format for statistical analysis, allowing for the accurate identification of demographic and injury patterns.

  3. ACTION (Methodology):

    I **used the R language** and its dedicated packages to implement robust data pipelines. This involved reading various data formats, standardizing variables, handling missing values, and generating the necessary counts and percentages to calculate incidence rates, prevalence, and mortality for different injury causes.

  4. RESULT (Outcome):

    The analysis successfully characterized SCI patterns, revealing that **traumatic SCIs accounted for 92.7%** of cases, primarily from **falls (56.3%)** and road traffic accidents (25.0%). The population was predominantly **male (85.7%)** and **paraplegia (58.9%)** was the most common condition. These key findings provide **critical, actionable data** to inform and target national SCI prevention and rehabilitation strategies.
